Jerimiah O'Donavan Rossa

Rossa was a member of the Irish Republican Brotherhood. Rossa was the first person to organize the first bombing by Irish republicans of English cities, also known as the dynamite campaign. Rossa became an active recruiter for the Irish Republican Brotherhood.

Croppy's Stone

Croppy’s Stone is located on Croppy’s Acre, a public park near downtown Dublin that is supposedly the location of a mass grave for Irish revolutionaries. The “Croppy” name comes from the nickname for the cropped haircut of the rioters. Long subject to British colonial rule, Irish history is littered with battles against oppression. This stone is a monument to those who lost their lives in the 1798 uprising.

Jim Larkin

James Larkin ( 28 January 1874 - 30 January 1947) was an Irish independence leader, trade union leader, and socialists. He became well known for organizing strikes among working people in 1913. Larkin lived in the United States for about a decade, raising funds for the movement at home. His statue resides on O’Connell St.
