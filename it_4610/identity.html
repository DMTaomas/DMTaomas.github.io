Henry Grattan

Grattan was an Irish politician and lawyer. Grattan campaigned for legislative freedom for the Irish Parliament in 18th century Britain. He in addition was a member of the Irish Parliament.

James Joyce

Joyce is named one of Ireland’s most influential and known writers. He is most famous for his work titled Ulysses, Dubliners, and novels A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man, and Finnegans Wake. He is known for his exploration of new literacy methods. All of his novels take place in Dublin.

Constance Lloyd

Constance Lloyd was the wife of Oscar Wilde, one of Ireland’s most famous poets and playwrights. She was involved in the Rational Dress Society. She also campaigned for women’s right to serve in parliament. She wrote various children’s books and translated many of his reviews of his work from the Dutch. Most of the time Constance’s name is associated with stories of her husband’s tragedy.

Luke Kelly

Luke Kelly (17 November 1940 - 30 January 1984) was born, raised, and died in Dublin. He became famous for his role in the folk music revival in Ireland, and often sang about working class struggles. Multiple statues of Kelly reside around Dublin, but this one sits near Grafton St, a symbol for the many buskers in the area.

Phil P Lynott

Phillip P Lynott (20 August 1949 - 4 January 1986) was an Irish musician, well known for his membership in the band “Thin Lizzy”. Lynott was born and died in England, but was raised in Dublin by his Irish Grandparents. One of the few black Irish celebrities of the time, Lynott was an icon for many. He sung, played the bass, and wrote music in his early years, and later attained fame with Thin Lizzy. In addition, Lynott wrote poetry. Unfortunately, he passed away at 36 due to heart complications. His statue lives near Grafton St in Dublin, overseeing the busking scene.

4 Provinces

The above image represents the four provinces of Ireland and this monumental statue is seen in the middle of the road in Dublin, Ireland. As we learned, Ireland was broken down into four provinces. The four Provinces of Ireland: Connaut (Connaught), Leinster, Munster, and Ulster were permanently set by the English administration of James I. The four provinces of Ireland no longer serve administrative or political purposes but function as historical and cultural entities.

George Salmon

(25 September 1819 – 22 January 1904) was a distinguished and influential Irish mathematician and Anglican theologian. After working in algebraic geometry for two decades, Salmon devoted the last forty years of his life to theology. His entire career was spent at Trinity College Dublin. O'Connell Bridge

O’Connell Bridge is one the most important bridges in Ireland and it represents a key passageway that splits Dublin into two sides completely. Originally known as the Carlisle bridge and designed by James Gandon, this bridge played an iconic role in linking the southern and northern parts of Ireland. Architecturally, it displays fine workmanship in the ashlar stonework and cast-iron lamp standards. It makes a significant contribution to the cityscape and is of considerable social importance as the heart of the city of Dublin and the key bridge over the Liffey.
