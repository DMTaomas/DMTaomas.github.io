Eason's Clock

The Eason Clock sits above the Eason flagship store on O’Connell St in downtown Dublin. Eason’s is a bookstore, and was started by Charles Eason in 1886. Since then, this store has been essential in providing literature to Irish people.

F.X. Buckley Butchers

F.X. Buckley branded stores are owned by the Buckley family, who have roots as butchers going back to the 16th century. This shop was opened at Moore St. in Dublin in 1930, and Francis Xavier Buckley went on to open multiple other butcher shops in Dublin. Now, the business has expanded to Steakhouses, bars, and butcher shops.

Mellon's

Mellons Shop and Deli was built in 1905, and has been part of the Mellon family since 1920. The shop is currently run by Colm Mellon, the 4th generation of Mellon. The shop is a fixture for locals, known for their coffee and cakes. This sign sits in front of their store, beckoning customers to come in.

Sean Lynch

Sean Lynch (born 22 September 1942) played Rugby for the Irish Rugby Union team. His son now runs the Swan bar, located in downtown Dublin. The Lynch family has owned the bar since 1937. This statue sits in the corner of the Swan Bar, overlooking patrons.

Sandymount

The Sandymount Hotel (originally the Mount Herbert Hotel) property was purchased and then opened in 1955 by George and Rosaleen Loughran. Ownership was passed to their son John, who now runs it with his son, Gerard. They have survived multiple recessions and remain one of the few family run hotels in the area.

Arthur Guinness

The image above is a representation of the famous Arthur Guinness signing a lease for the St. James's Gate Brewery, Dublin. He leased the brewery for 9000 years at an annual rent of £45. The brewery was only 4 acres in size, disused, and had little brewing equipment. This Guinness barrel represents one of the many barrels around Dublin showing respect to Arthur and his brewery craft. Due to Guinness, now being distributed worldwide, Ireland is known for this specific type of brew and this small town man turned this into a million dollar corporation.
